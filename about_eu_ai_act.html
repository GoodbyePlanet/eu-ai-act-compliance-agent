[ EU AI ACT BRIEF ]
Regulation Overview

About the EU AI Act

The EU AI Act is a risk-based regulation for AI systems used in the EU market. It classifies AI use cases by risk level and applies obligations proportionally, with the strictest obligations on high-risk systems and outright bans for unacceptable-risk practices.

Risk Tiers

  • Unacceptable Risk: Prohibited use cases.
  • High Risk: Strong governance, documentation, and oversight obligations.
  • Limited Risk: Transparency duties (for example, user-facing disclosures).
  • Minimal Risk: Generally no additional mandatory requirements under the Act.

Who Is Impacted

Providers, deployers, importers, and distributors of AI systems may all have obligations depending on role, market placement, and use case context.

Typical High-Risk Obligations

  • Risk management process and technical documentation.
  • Data governance and quality controls.
  • Logging, monitoring, and incident handling.
  • Human oversight and user instructions.
  • Conformity assessment and post-market obligations.
